Egg Fried Rice

Like the classic Fried Rice, this rice dish is a Chinese delicacy. It is made up mainly of rice, eggs, vegetables, and spices. Egg Fried Rice is very flexible and easy to make. It is a great meal to quickly and easily whip up with leftover white rice and ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 5 eggs
  • 5 cups white rice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric (or curry powder)
  • 300g of mixed vegetables (Carrots, Peas, Spring Onions, Red/Green Bell Peppers)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar (optional)
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 Seasoning Cubes
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Instructions

  1. Wash and boil your rice with a little salt until it is well cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Beat 2 eggs in one bowl and set aside; this will be used to garnish the food (but you can skip this and mix all the eggs if you prefer not to garnish).
  3. Beat the other 3 eggs in another bowl, along with 2 tablespoons water, black pepper, salt, and seasoning. Set aside.
  4. Heat a pan over medium heat and add 2 tablespoons of oil. Pour the 2 beaten eggs (the one without the spices) and scramble them. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  5. Then heat the pan over high heat and add 2 tablespoons of oil. Add the diced onion and mixed vegetables. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Next, add the rice and stir-fry for 2 minutes, stirring the rice uniformly to break up any clumps.
  7. Then, pour the uncooked egg (the one with the spice mixture) over the rice, and stir-fry for about 1 minute, until all the rice grains are coated in egg. Sprinkle in vinegar if you want to get rid of the egg smell.
  8. Stir-fry continuously for another minute. Next add soy sauce, garlic, salt, seasoning and chili sauce over the rice and mix.
  9. If the rice looks a little dry, sprinkle in some water or chicken stock.
  10. Mix in the scrambled eggs to garnish.
  11. Keep stir-frying lightly for a final 2-3 minutes until cooked through and serve.

Pro tips to Note

  • Butter will give your rice the very best flavor. But if you prefer, you can use just oil.
  • Rice should be cooked ahead and allowed to cool(if you prefer using cooked rice).
  • Use any veggies you like; fresh instead of frozen.
